# AOS v3.1 System Cleanup & Consistency Restoration

**Date:** 2026-03-05
**Agent:** God (Orchestrator)

## 🎯 Objective
To eliminate technical debt caused by the transition from `dispatch-v2` / `ATO` to **AOS v3.1**, and to enforce a unified file structure that reflects the "Kernel-Effector-Interface" architecture.

## 🛠️ Key Actions

### 1. Directory Consolidation (`skills/aos`)
Merged the legacy `agent-orchestration-system` skill into the user-facing `aos` skill to create a single source of truth.
- **Kernel (`core/`)**: `autopilot`, `dispatch_router`, `queue_sync`
- **Effectors (`effectors/`)**: `heartbeat_full`, `spawn_runner`, `execute_actions`
- **Interface (`cli/`)**: `status`, `sprint`, `review`

### 2. Cron Job Restoration
Identified that the active **AOS Heartbeat** cron job was pointing to deleted paths (`skills/agent-orchestration-system/...`).
- **Fix**: Updated the cron payload to point to the new `skills/aos/scripts/core/` and `skills/aos/scripts/effectors/` paths.
- **Result**: Prevented a silent failure of the automation pipeline.

### 3. Agent Identity Alignment
Discovered that `SOUL.md` and `IDENTITY.md` files for sub-agents (COO, CTO, CMO, Reviewer) were missing from their runtime directories.
- **Action**: Deployed the definitive prompts from the repository to `agents/<role>/`.
- **Impact**: Ensured agents correctly embody their AOS v3.1 roles (e.g., "Read-Only Runner", "Deterministic Merge").

## 🧠 Reflections
The system's resilience was tested during this refactor. The ability to "hot-fix" the running cron job without a restart demonstrates the power of the OpenClaw architecture. Moving forward, directory structure changes must be immediately propagated to `openclaw.json` configurations.
